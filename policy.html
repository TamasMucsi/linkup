LinkUp Community Policy & Guidelines

Last updated: October 9, 2025

Welcome to LinkUp, a community-driven space where people come together to share stories, links, ideas, and conversations. To keep LinkUp open, welcoming, and safe for everyone, we’ve built a few ground rules. By using LinkUp, you agree to follow these policies, along with our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

1. Community Guidelines

LinkUp thrives on respect, curiosity, and creativity. Here’s what’s expected of every member:

1.1 Respect Others

  • Be kind. No harassment, hate speech, threats, or bullying.
  • Do not post content that discriminates based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, nationality, or disability.
  • Attack ideas, not people.

1.2 Keep It Real

  • Don’t spam, self-promote excessively, or post repetitive content.
  • Don’t use bots or fake accounts to manipulate votes, trends, or engagement.
  • Don’t impersonate other users, public figures, or LinkUp staff.

1.3 Follow the Law

  • Do not post, share, or encourage illegal activities.
  • No pirated materials, stolen content, or anything that violates copyright law.
  • Never share private or identifying information about others without consent (“doxxing”).

1.4 NSFW & Sensitive Content

  • NSFW (Not Safe For Work) posts are allowed in designated communities only — and must be clearly labeled.
  • NSFW content must not include nudity involving minors, sexual exploitation, or non-consensual acts.
  • Communities can choose to disallow NSFW material entirely.

1.5 Age Requirement

  • You must be 13 years or older to use LinkUp.
  • Certain areas of the site may require you to be 18+.

2. Moderation Policy

Moderation keeps LinkUp safe and constructive. Our moderation system has two layers — community moderators and the LinkUp Trust & Safety team.

2.1 Community Moderators

  • Each community (“Link”) has volunteer moderators who set and enforce their local rules.
  • Mods can remove posts, issue bans, and lock threads.
  • Moderators are expected to act fairly, transparently, and within the bounds of LinkUp’s global policy.

2.2 Platform Enforcement

  • The LinkUp team may step in for serious or repeated violations.
  • Actions may include warnings, temporary suspensions, or permanent bans.
  • If you believe an action was taken in error, you can appeal by contacting appeals@linkup.com

3. Content Ownership & Copyright

3.1 You Own Your Content

  • Anything you post on LinkUp is yours.
  • By posting, you grant LinkUp a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to display, distribute, and host your content as part of the platform’s operation.

3.2 Copyright Infringement (DMCA)

If you believe your copyrighted work has been posted without permission, send a DMCA notice to: legal@linkup.com

Your message must include:

  • Your full contact details
  • A description of the copyrighted material
  • The exact URL(s) of the infringing content
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on their behalf

4. Privacy & Data Use

We respect your privacy.

Our Privacy Policy explains what information we collect and how we use it, but here’s the short version:

  • We only collect data necessary to run and improve the service.
  • We don’t sell your personal information.
  • You can download or delete your data anytime.
  • We may use anonymized data to analyze trends and improve features.

5. Reporting Problems

If you see content or behavior that violates these policies:

  • Use the Report button under the post or comment.
  • Contact report@linkup.com for urgent issues (like threats, self-harm, or illegal content).
  • Reports are reviewed by moderators and the Trust & Safety team as quickly as possible.

6. Enforcement & Penalties

Depending on the nature and frequency of a violation, possible actions include:

  • Removal of posts or comments
  • Temporary suspension of your account
  • Permanent account ban
  • Community-specific bans
  • IP or device restrictions (for repeated or severe offenses)

We may remove any content or account that poses a risk to user safety, the community, or the platform’s integrity.

7. Transparency

We believe in accountability.

LinkUp periodically publishes a Transparency Report outlining:

  • The number of removed posts and banned accounts
  • Categories of violations
  • Government or legal requests for data
  • Appeal outcomes

8. Updates to This Policy

We may modify these guidelines over time to reflect new laws, features, or community feedback. Major updates will be announced on the site and via email before taking effect.
